## 👋 Welcome!

If you're here, you're probably learning to code. Maybe you're excited. Maybe you're overwhelmed. Maybe both.

**This is a judgment-free zone.** We go slow. We explain things properly. We build confidence, not frustration.

---

## 🎯 Who This Is For

- **Absolute beginners** who've never written code before
- **Career-changers** moving into tech/AI from other fields
- **Anyone who's tried to learn before** but got stuck on confusing jargon
- **People who learn better** with real-world examples, not abstract theory

If you've ever thought, "Why can't they just explain this normally?" — you're in the right place.

---

## 🧠 How This Works

Each lesson follows the same format:

1. **Plain English Explanation** — No jargon without translation
2. **Real-World Analogy** — Connect new concepts to things you already know
3. **Code Examples** — Simple, clear, copy-paste ready
4. **Practice Exercise** — Try it yourself (with solutions provided)
5. **Quick Recap** — Key points to remember

**Pace:** 3-4 lessons per week. Go at your own speed. Review as needed.

---

## 🚀 How to Use This

1. **Start with Lesson 1** (Python or SQL, depending on your track)
2. **Read the lesson** — take your time
3. **Try the exercise** — don't peek at the solution!
4. **Check your answer** — see how you did
5. **Move to the next lesson** when you feel ready

**Tip:** It's okay to re-read lessons. It's okay to take breaks. Learning isn't a race.

## 🌟 Progress Tracking

Want to track your progress? Here's a simple way:

**Create a learning journal** (Google Doc, Notion, or even a notebook):
- Write down what you learned each lesson
- Note what was confusing (and what made it click)
- Celebrate small wins

**Milestone idea:** After every 5 lessons, build something small with what you've learned.

## 🙏 A Note on Learning

**It's normal to feel confused.** Coding is a new way of thinking. Your brain needs time to build new pathways.

**It's normal to make mistakes.** Errors are not failures — they're information. They tell you what to try next.

**It's normal to need time.** Some concepts click immediately. Others take weeks. Both are fine.

**You belong here.** The tech world needs diverse perspectives, not just people who've coded since age 12.
